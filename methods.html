Molecular Dynamics
Molecular dynamics simulation can provide great insight about the properties of biomolecules at the nanometric scales. In my research, I have used enhanced molecular dynamics simulations to study DNA melting, multiple walker metadynamics to assess the affinity of single stranded molecules to hydrophobic surfaces, and also obtained elastic properties of different molecules just from equilibrium simulations.

Enhanced molecular dynamics simulation of DNA melting in the presence of an AC field.

Multiple-walker metadynamics simulation assessing the conformation of ssDNA in proximity to a hydrophobic surface.

Finite Element Method
Finite element simulations are extremely useful to design and optimize biosensors. In my research, I have used FEM simulations to assess current drops in nanopore devices, optimize membrane sensors for protein detection , and studied field leakage through dielectric materials.

Current drops obtained from the FEM modeling of a nanopore device.

Analytical Kinetics
Simulations themselves may be interesting, but to really understand a phenomenon one has to focus on the underlying physics. Poisson-Nernst-Planck and Navier-Stokes equations are fundamental for the study of ion dynamics at the micro and nano-scale level, and my latest research consists on solving these equations under different boundary conditions.
