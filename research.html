Impedance-Based Biosensors:
Design and optimization of biosensors is essential for early detection of diseases such as cancer, infectious diseases and autoimmune diseases. Due to their high throughput, high sensitivity and ease of fabrication, impedance-based biosensors are trending both in academia and in labs all over the world. I’m especially interested in understanding the fundamentals of how these sensors work, as I believe proper understanding of the physics behind them can enhance their optimization and make them more useful for clinical applications.

DNA / RNA Dynamics :
Detection and cure of many diseases may lie before the information is passed into proteins, in nucleic acids. Small RNA molecules called miRNAs are the mail-carriers of our immune system, and I believe counting or hijacking them may help in the diagnose or cure or of many diseases (especially autoimmune ones). With this in mind, I have special interest in DNA dynamics, as the knowledge of DNA-DNA or DNA-protein interactions can assist on drug design, on the development of biosensors, and can provide useful information about many processes happening inside our bodies as well.

Protein Aggregation
Precipitation of amyloid-beta peptides has been suggested to be responsible for Alzheimer. With this in mind, more knowledge about the factors that influence protein precipitation can lead to better understanding of how this devastating disease works. In the future, I would like to study protein precipitation and analyze how different parameters of the local environment (confinement, ionic solutions, etc.) can affect it.
